### Controversial Decision: Heinrich Klaasen's Dismissal (14th Over)

**The Controversy:**

- **Context:** Klaasen was set on 31 runs, anchoring SRH's recovery
- **Event:** Klaasen launched a powerful shot toward the boundary that appeared destined for six
- **What Happened:** Phil Salt intercepted the ball at the boundary while losing his footing and balance
- **The Question:** Did Salt's back foot/body touch the boundary cushion BEFORE or AFTER securing the catch?

**DRS Review Details:**
- **Fielder:** Phil Salt (RCB)
- **Third Umpire Review:** Rohan Pandit reviewed the decision
- **Umpire's Call:** "I don't see any movement of the cushions" - Klaasen ruled OUT
- **Replays:** Showed questionable contact between Salt and the boundary padding while falling backward
- **Viewer Perception:** Estimated 99.99% of viewers thought it was a six

**Player Reaction:**
- Klaasen was visibly agitated and fuming at the decision
- Engaged in heated exchange with 4th umpire near boundary rope
- Lingered to argue his case after dismissal

**Expert Commentary:**
- Former Indian Captain Kris Srikkanth stated: "It was a great catch, but everybody thought his back foot touched the rope. I am sure 99.99% of the viewers thought it was a six. It was an absurd decision. The cushion is moving, so I'm not sure what the third umpire saw"
- Controversy centered on whether the boundary cushion moved during the catch

**Match Impact:**
- In the next 4.4 overs after Klaasen's dismissal, SRH lost 4 MORE WICKETS
- Scoring 200 became nearly impossible after this turning point
- SRH fell from strong recovery position to losing momentum
